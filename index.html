From the Country Representative's Desk

Wishing you a very Happy New Year! As we step into 2025, we are delighted to share the very first quarterly newsletter, highlighting recent accomplishments and upcoming activities under the Himalaya for the Future (HFF).

 

IUCN-TATA initiative, 'Himalaya for the Future' (HFF), supported by the TCS Foundation, TATA Group, aims to develop a strong basis for nature-based solutions underpinning development, which will result in a significant legacy of improved social, economic, and environmental conditions in the Himalayan region. 

 

The insights, recommendations, and groundwork laid during Phase I have provided the momentum for Himalaya for the Future Phase II (2024–2026). Key activities for this phase include communication strategies, networking efforts to engage stakeholders, offering media fellowships, documenting nature-based solutions (NbS) case studies, organizing International Mountain Day event, and producing a short film to highlight the journey and impact of the initiative. 

 

We are delighted to share that Phase II began on a high note with the successful celebration of International Mountain Day in collaboration with GB Pant National Institute of Himalayan Environment, under the aegis of the Ministry of Environment, Forest, and Climate Change. A major highlight this year is Youth4Himalaya, an experiential learning initiative that aims to inspire and empower the younger generation and will expand to schools across all 13 Himalayan States/UTs. 

We look forward to continuing this important journey together and sharing more updates in the months ahead.

 

With warm wishes for a fruitful 2025.

 

Yash Veer Bhatnagar, PhD

Mapping Potential OECMs in High Altitude Indian Himalayan Region

IUCN India is implementing a project on "Mapping potential OECMs in high altitude Indian Himalayan Region” in 2025. OECMs are geographically defined areas distinct from traditional Protected Areas (PAs) but managed in ways that yield positive, sustained, and long-term outcomes for biodiversity conservation, including associated ecosystem functions, services, and, when applicable, cultural, spiritual, socio-economic, and other locally significant values. The project aims to conduct a study on the identification and mapping potential other effective area-based conservation measures (OECMs) in the Indian Himalayan region (with focus on high altitude region). 
 
Since the Himalayas in India offer unique opportunity for expansion of PA network, as the Indian Himalayan Region (IHR) harbours two global biodiversity hotspots, following IUCN-WCPA’s (2019) and UNDP (2022) guidelines for identification of the OECMs, the OECMs for the IHR will be identified and mapped on spatial domain using ArcGIS. 
 

Accelerating Community-based Interventions for Forest Fire Prevention and Management (ACT4FIRE)

IUCN and UNDP India are developing a project on ‘Accelerating Community-based Interventions for Forest Fire Prevention and Management in selected landscapes of Uttarakhand and Jharkhand (ACT4FIRE)’ under the guidance of IGF, Forest Protection Division, MoEFCC that addresses Land Degradation and Climate Change. The proposed project aimed to complement the ‘National Action Plan on Forest Fires’, prepared by MoEFCC. The plan comprises taking actions by informing, enabling and empowering forest fringe communities and incentivizing them to work in tandem with the Forest Departments, while also reducing vulnerability of forests against fire hazards, enhancing the capabilities of forest personnel and institutions in fighting fires, and speeding up recovery after a fire event. The proposed project will focus on the States of Uttarakhand and Jharkhand, which as per ISFR 2021 were among the top ten Forest Fire Prone states. Heavy dependence of people on natural resources has also led to severe degradation of forest quality in certain areas. Considering the significant ecosystem services that this mountain landscape provides to the people of Uttarakhand, quantitative evaluation of its values and measures to arrest the degradation of forested habitats triggered by forest fires is critical.
 

CEDAR Workshop

Meenal Pahuja, Senior Project Associate- Habitats & Species, represented IUCN India in a workshop on "Springshed management in the Himalayas" on October 23, 2024 in Dehradun. The jointly organized workshop by Himmotthan Society (Tata Trusts' associate organizations) and CEDAR aimed to 

focus on awareness building and knowledge sharing in the field of water resources development in mountain regions, with specific emphasis on the field level skills for improved planning and implementation of subsurface and spring conservation activities.
